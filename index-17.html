EARLY LIFE

  • Thomas Jefferson was born into the planter class in the state of Virginia
  • Graduated from College of William & Mary
  • He is most famously known for being the main author of the Declaration of Independence
  • He was a Democratic-Republican and believed in a small government
  • Before becoming President, he was Secretary of State (1790-1793) and Vice President (1797-1801)
Slide 2

ELECTION OF 1800

  • Election between the Federalists (John Adams and Charles Pinckney) and the Democratic-Republicans (Thomas Jefferson and Aaron Burr)
  • Back then, electors cast 2 votes
  • Jefferson and Burr had a tie of 73-73 (even though they were in the same party)
  • Burr refused to step aside, letting the House of Representatives decide
  • Hamilton convinced the Federalists to vote for Jefferson
  • Jefferson became president and Burr became Vice President
  • Jefferson never trusted Burr again
  • The 12th Amendment (1804) was passed later to avoid further ties like this
Slide 3

JEFFERSON'S PRESIDENCY

  • Jefferson was very popular in his first term
  • Jefferson reduced the national debt
  • He removed all internal taxes (including the whiskey tax)
  • Jefferson bought Louisiana Territory for $15 million from France (very popular)
  • The Tripoli wars (1801-1805) were popular but costly
  • Unlike 1800, for the 1804 election Jefferson won by a landslide due to his popularity
  • In his second term he was not as successful and decided not to run for president again
  • The Embargo Act (1807) forbade all trade with other countries
  • The Embargo Act devastated the American Economy
  • He was considered one of the best presidents but also had many controversies

JUDICIAL REVIEW

  • Judiciary Act of 1801
  • Passed after the election but before Jefferson took office
  • During the last days of his presidency, to keep federalist power in courts, John Adams appointed hundreds of justices to the Supreme Court
  • Marbury vs Madison (1803)
  • William Marbury was not commissioned
  • Supreme Court case where John Marshall established judicial review
  • Allowed the judicial branch to declare laws unconstitutional and made the constitution the supreme law
